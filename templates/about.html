ABOUT THE ARTIST

Piet Mondrian

was a Dutch painter renowned for his role in developing the De Stijl art

movement, which emphasized simplicity and abstraction. He is best known for his abstract

works that use a grid of vertical and horizontal black lines and the three primary colors: red,

yellow, and blue. Mondrian's art evolved over his career, but he is most celebrated for these

geometric compositions, where he reduced forms and colors to their most basic elements.

His work not only had a profound influence on abstract art but also on architecture, design,

and fashion, reflecting his vision of art's role in achieving harmony and balance in life.
